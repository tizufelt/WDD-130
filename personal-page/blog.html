Produced from 1973 to 1987, Square Body trucks are the third generation of the Chevrolet and GMC C/K pickups. The Square Body moniker comes from their box-like shape, despite being named the “Rounded-Line” generation by Chevy. The trucks feature a rounded windshield, door frames, and windows that contrast the box-body design. The Square Body trucks were one of Chevy’s longest-running generations. With a 14-year production span, they aren’t as rare as other C10 generations, but they are easier to find. Their accessibility and affordability have made them popular choices for restorations and restomods.
